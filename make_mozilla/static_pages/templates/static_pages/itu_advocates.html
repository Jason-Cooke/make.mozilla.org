Grantees

Your Allies In The Fight For The Open Web

Mozilla has funded ten open web advocates from around the world to get involved and raise awareness about the ITU. They’re working on a number of different projects that may fit with your message, and many of them will be on the ground in Dubai. They're great allies if you have an idea and are looking for collaborators, or if you're looking to help with a project that is already moving ahead.

To make it easy to search through, we've included a description of the projects by region and included the contact information of the people coordinating them below.

Get in touch! If you'd like to contact any of the advocates who do not have their e-mails listed, send a message to tim@mozillafoundation.org.

Africa

Romain Abile Houehou - Engaging African Consumers in Open Internet

Organization:

African Ict Consumer Network (AICN)

Location:

Benin

Project:

Sensitizing consumer organizations of Internet services to push for their governments to support open Internet. The AICN will make available in French version the Baku IGF meeting report titled "Proposed ITR Revisions and Their Implications for Freedom of Expression Online" to sensitize consumers organizations from the 14 francophone countries of West Africa on the positions they may adopt when facing their governments concerning Dubai World Conference on International Telecommunications (WCIT). Following this action that will occur in the lead up to the conference, we will get into contact with African organizations on the ground in Dubai for organizing field actions during the conference.

Jimson Olufuye - Participation in the Nigerian Delegation to WCIT

E-mail:

info@aficta.org

Organization:

Africa ICT Alliance (AfICTA)

Location:

Nigeria

Project:

Jimson will be traveling as part of the Nigerian delegation to WCIT. He will be attending in support of the AfLCTA position and joining others to push for same or similarly progressive positions.

John Walubengo - Engage With The Kenya Government on the ITRs

E-mail:

jwalubengo@mmu.ac.ke

Location:

Project:

John will be attending the WCIT as a member of the Kenyan delegation. He will be playing an advisory role in supporting open, multistakeholder approaches.

Asia

Isriya Paireepairit - "How The Internet is Regulated" Article Series

E-mail:

markpeak@blognone.com

Organization:

Blognone

Location:

Thailand

Project:

Blognone.com is a leading Thai technology news site. Isriya will be producing a series of articles on how the Internet is regulated in Thai to explain the issues around the ITU/WCIT/ITR and their significance to Thai Internet users.

Shyam Ponappa - Participation in the Indian Delegation to WCIT

E-mail:

shyamponappa@gmail.com

Organization:

Centre for Internet and Society

Location:

India

Project:

Shyam is a member of the India delegation to WCIT. Mr. Ponappa will be attending to advocate for CIS’ view that the amendments to the ITRs be restricted to telecommunications services and telecommunications transport means. The Centre for Internet and Society (CIS) has a keen interest in participating in this conference in order to ensure a greater representation of civil society and try and prevent any undue expansion of the ITU’s role in internet governance without proper participation by all the stakeholders involved.

Babu Ram Aryal - Panel Discussion on Nepalese Amendments at WCIT

E-mail:

babu@isoc.org.np

Organization:

ISOC Nepal

Location:

Nepal

Project:

Babu will be coordinating a discussion prior to WCIT with government representatives, ISOC members, and representatives in private industry to discuss the Nepalese position on the proposed amendments to be discussed in Dubai.

Ahmed Swapan Mahmud - Public Dialogue on WCIT in Bangladesh

Organization:

VOICE and ISOC

Location:

Bangladesh

Project:

Ahmed will be organizing a public meeting on the eve of WCIT. This will be a public meeting with multi-stakeholder approach. They will invite government, law and policy makers, IT business companies, ISPs, mobile operators, human rights and media organizations, journalists, academicians, NGOs and CSOs The purpose is to raise public understanding on the proposed ITR amendments and their implications for freedom of expression online.

Europe

Elena Zvarici - Inform and Engage on Romania's WCIT Position

E-mail:

elena.zvarici@isoc.ro

Organization:

Internet Society Romania

Location:

Romania

Project:

Elena will be launching an article and accompanying infographic targeted at Romania's Internet community and decisionmakers to inform them on what the WCIT is, what will be discussed at the upcoming meeting, and how people can be involved in the process. Her aim is to advocate for a future with a more open, multistakeholder, participative process for discussing and contributing to Internet governance related issues.

South America

Francisco Vera Hott - ¿Qué pasa en ITU?

E-mail:

francisco@derechosdigitales.org

Organization:

ONG Derechos Digitales

Location:

Chile

Project:

Derechos Digitales is an independent, non-profit and non-governmental organization, founded in 2004, whose mission is to promote the defense and protection of human rights in the digital environment. They will set up a small website that work as a resource center related with ITU issues for Spanish speaking people, governments and organizations. The website will contain key documents and other material related with the ITU, translated and localized in Spanish.
