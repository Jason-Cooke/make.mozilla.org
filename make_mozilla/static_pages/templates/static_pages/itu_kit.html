ITU Engagement Kit

So you’ve heard about the ITU, and want to get involved in spreading awareness about the meeting in Dubai? You’ve come to the right place!

This Engagement Kit provides resources for understanding what’s at stake and the tools to make a difference. It’s all about advocating for the rights of everyday web users to ensure we all have a voice in how the Internet is governed. This page will help you build a Popcorn video you can use to get people involved.

How It Works

  • Step 1 - Choose the issue that matters most to you
  • Step 2 - Decide what you want people to do about it
  • Step 3 - Create and share your message.

Step 1 – Choose the Issue that Matters Most to You

The upcoming meeting of the ITU is called the World Conference of International Telecommunications (WCIT). It's set to run from December 3-14, 2012, in Dubai. There are a lot of issues up for discussion. Use this section to figure out the ones that matter most to you.

For a quick summary overview of what the ITU is and why this meeting of the WCIT is important, check out:

  • 'What Is The ITU.' A four-minute video rundown from Access Now and Fight for the Future.
  • The Internet Society has this quick description on why the WCIT meeting is important.

Step 2 – Decide What You Want People to Do

Now that you know what's at stake, what do you want people – your friends, colleagues, or community members – to do about it? Choose from the options we've pulled together or create you own.

You can ask people to…

A) Contact Their ITU Delegation

Governments will be represented at the ITU by delegations from each country. These people will take part in the meetings, so ensuring they hear your voice is a good place to start.

An international coalition of civil society groups has launched a tool that will let you send an e-mail directly to your country's ITU delegation. You can access it here.

B) Mobilize on the Web

An international coalition of advocates and activists are bringing together as many web users as possible to make a joint declaration to the ITU for greater transparency and participation. Signing and spreading the word about these petitions will send a clear message.

  • OpenMedia.ca: "Protect Internet Freedom."Asserts that Internet governance decisions "should be made in a transparent manner with genuine multi-stakeholder participation from civil society, governments, and the private sector".
  • Access Now & Fight For The Future: "What Is The ITU?" A rallying cry for individuals on the ground to demand that their governments not hand over web governance functions to the ITU.
  • Center for Democracy and Technology: "Open Letter". Emphasizes the human rights issues surrounding the ITU's lack of transparency. Open to organizational sign-ons. Translated into multiple languages.
  • Google: "Take Action". “A free and open world depends on a free and open Internet. Governments alone, working behind closed doors, should not direct its future.”
  • Best Bits: &"Statement of Civil Society Groups and Members". A coalition of international civil society groups. This letter includes a number of specific policy statements and recommendations to the ITU.

C) Organize a Local Event

Mobilizing people on the ground in your community is a great way to engage in the conversation. Mozilla's Webmaker platform makes it easy for you to schedule your own event and let others know about it. You can also collaborate with:

  • Mozilla Representatives ("ReMo"). ReMo is a coalition of volunteer Mozillians around the world. They're plugged into local communities engaged with these issues, and are a good group to get in touch with if you're planning something.
  • Mozilla Micro-Grantees. We've funded ten open web advocates from around the world to get involved and raise awareness about the ITU. They're working on a number of different projects that may fit with your message, and some of them will be on the ground in Dubai. Get in touch with them here.

Step 3 – Create and Share Your Message

You've chosen the issues you care about and you've decided what you want your friends to do about it. Now, you can craft and customize your own Popcorn video to get the word out. Some things to consider when creating your message:

  • What do you think about the WCIT and ITU? What concerns you the most?
  • How do you want the web to be governed?
  • Target Audience: Who needs to hear about the ITU? Who can you get involved?
  • Local Needs: What will make the ITU relevant to your local community?
  • Call to Action: What do you want people to do? How will they do it?
  • Sharing: How do you make the message sharable? Is there someone that others can do to help you spread the word?
  • Language: Have you translated the video into your local language?

In collaboration with Access Now and Fight for the Future, we've put together an interactive video about the ITU that you can customize for your own needs and share with others. Just click here and you'll be editing your own video in minutes.

If making a Popcorn video isn't your thing you could write a blog post, share your message on social media, or just start talking to your friends in your local community about what's going on.

Finally: Activate! Get involved and share your message with others.
