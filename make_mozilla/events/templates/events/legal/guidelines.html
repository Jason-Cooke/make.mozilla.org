Event Guidelines; Being Safe on the Events Platform

This publication is intended as a general guide and is not to be taken as professional advice.

Planning Safe Events

  • Is this the right space? This is not the place to announce private and personal events such as birthday parties
  • Events may be public: Event information may show up in public searches, don’t submit information that you don’t want to be shared publicly (i.e.: phone number and address)
  • Who are you reaching out to? Be aware of your audience both on the Events Platform and at your event.
  • Who uses the Events Platform? The events platform is meant for a broad range of users - please keep your content appropriate. See the Content Guidelines for details
  • Venue: Make sure your venue is the right choice (i.e.: size, location and accessibility)
  • Releases: If you plan on taking and using recordings, pictures and/or video, be sure to secure releases from your participants

If You are Attending an Event

  • Location: Know where you are going and how you are getting to and from
  • Be safe: Do you know who is putting on the event, especially if it is at a private residence?

Special Consideration for Minors

  • Parent involvement: If you are planning an event, keep parents involved, including, if appropriate, that parents accompany their children to the event and sign consent forms
  • Interaction between adults and minors: Make sure everyone respects each other’s personal space and that individual adults who are not legal guardians of a minor are not left alone with that minor
  • Venue and time: If you expect minors to attend make sure the venue and time are age appropriate
