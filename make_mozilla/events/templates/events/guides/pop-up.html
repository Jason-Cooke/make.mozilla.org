Create an event

Overview

A Kitchen Table event is a fun way to gather with friends at home around your kitchen table to hang out and make cool things on the web.

All you need are a few friends to join you, a place to meet, and something you want to learn or make online.

This guide will help!

Facts and figures

For a kitchen table event you're looking at about:

  • 1-5 friends
  • One hour
  • At home or in a cafe

Before

Decide what you want to make

We’ve built some fun projects to get you started with making on the web. You can follow these ideas or swap in whatever activities you like.

Be sure to think about the interests and skill levels of your group. Adapt accordingly!

Find a space and a date

Find a comfortable, flexible space for the event. Your kitchen or living room are good, or a nearby cafe. Make sure you can move the chairs and sit together in a circle.

Think about wifi and power, good lighting, and snacks.

Pick a date. Give yourself enough time and make sure the date works for your friends. An hour during the evening or on the weekend, for example, is often a good choice.

Invite your friends
Write a short invitation.

Want to remix a website? Join me in LOCATION, DATE.

I'm going to learn how play with code & thought you might want to, too!

On DATE we're hanging out in LOCATION to make something together online.

There will be yummy snacks, hacking, and the company of friends.

Bring a computer, if you can.

Let me know if you can come.

— Sincerely, YOU

You can also customize this sample invitation using the Hackasaurus X-Ray Goggles.

During

Introduce your event

When your friends arrive, make sure everyone is comfortable.

Do a quick go round and introduce people.

Give a brief overview of the day.

Try out a learning project

Show your friends the [learning project] you selected. Demonstrate the first few steps so they get the idea.

Now let your friends try.

Check in with everyone, answer any questions, and make sure the group is ready before going to the next step.

Keep hacking

If someone wants to try something else, help them select another learning project. Sometimes it’s hard to know how fast a group will go through a project, so it’s good to have a few options prepared.

You can also find other activities online to fit their interests and skill level.

Check in with your friends to make sure they're doing alright.

Share your hacks

Hit “Publish” when you’re finished with a hack, or take a screenshot or photo.

If you like, share your hacks with the world!

Add a picture of the hack to a gallery online and tag it with #mozparty. You can use Flickr, Picasa, Facebook, Twitter or other photosharing sites to do this.

Tweet the URL and tag it #mozparty or paste the URL in this etherpad.

Show your friends what you made and ask to see their hacks, too.

After

Wrap Up
  • Ask everyone what they learned. Encourage them to give feedback about the event. If they have suggestions for making it better, be sure to take notes.
  • Talk about whether you want to keep hacking together. You may want to host another kitchen table event to keep playing with these projects or to try something else. Talk about what your friends would like to do next.
Set a date for the next one
  • If there’s interest to do so, set the date and location for the next event!
  • Let us know how it went. We’ll email you after the event to collect your feedback. If your participants agree and sign up for it, we’ll also email them to ask how it went.
  • If there's enough interest, pick a date and location for the next time!
  • If you hosted the event, we’ll email you afterward to hear how it went and get your feedback. We’ll also email your guests, if they signed up and agreed to be contacted.
