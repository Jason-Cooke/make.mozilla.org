Overview

A Pop Up event:

  • Brings together local organizations
  • to host learning stations that participants can sample.

This guide will help!

It's a community document, so please share your ideas. You can also see what other hosts did.

Before

Decide what you want to make
  • Read our guidelines on planning safe events
  • Recruit a handful of organizations interested in webmaking.
  • Each organization will host a learning station, i.e. a simple table-top activity, based on their programs.
  • You can also use these webmaker projectsas ideas.
  • Agree on shared goals and stations in a collaborative document.
Find a space and a date
  • Find a comfortable, flexible space. You'll need several tables for each station and room to move about.
  • Think about wifi and power, good lighting, and snacks.
  • Pick a date. Give yourself enough time and make sure the date works for the organizations.
Spread the word
  • Write a short invitation. Use the shared document as a basis.
  • Encourage the participating organizations to invite their communities.
  • Create a page for your event, and share the link widely.

During

Welcome everyone
  • Set up tables in a large circle. Use posters or other signage to indicate the stations.
  • Do a quick go-round.Ask participants to introduce themselves and the stations.
  • Give a brief overview. Explain how the stations will work and what's in store for the day.
Visit stations
  • Participants sample the stations.
  • Encourage them to follow their interests, either staying at stations as long as they like or moving on.
  • Remind facilitators to welcome newcomers and keep a good flow.
Share your hacks
  • After an hour or two, bring the group into a circle.
  • Invite a few participants to share what they made.
  • Keep hacking at the stations, if participants are interested.
  • If you like, please share your hacks with the world!
    • Paste the URL in this etherpad
    • or tweet the hacks and tag it #mozparty
    • or upload a picture to a gallery, such as Flickr, and tag it with #mozparty.

After

Wrap Up
  • Ask everyone how it went. Encourage feedback about the event and the projects. What did they learn? What did they like? If they have suggestions for making it better, be sure to take notes or invite them to blog about it.
  • Talk about continuing to hack together. You may want to keep playing with these projects or to try new ones later. Talk about what the participants would like to do next, such as organize another event.
  • Set a date. If there's interest, pick a date for the next time!
